Sophie ran down the corridor. She was quite frantic as she heard the constant moaning and wailing of the air raid sirens, peppered by distant explosions intermittently. She ran to her desk, pulling out the drawer in a hurry. The contents spilled onto the floor, and she sifted through it with her delicate, pale hands, desperately searching for her father’s watch, the one he gave her before he was deployed on the Eastern front in the great war.
She found it.
The hands of the watch were bent, and the time read, 7:24, the exact time it was dropped almost a decade ago during an unfortunate cricket match. 
“Found you” she exclaimed, before she went to join her neighbours in the local bunker.
